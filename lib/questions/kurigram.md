=== KURIGRAM - QUIZ DATA ===

Category: Places & History

[MCQ] Kurigram is located in which administrative division?
A) Rajshahi
B) Rangpur
C) Sylhet
D) Mymensingh
CORRECT ANSWER: B) Rangpur

[TRUE/FALSE] Kurigram is known as a riverine district with many "Chars" (river islands).
CORRECT ANSWER: True

[MCQ] Which major river flows through Kurigram?
A) Brahmaputra/Jamuna, Dharla, and Teesta
B) Padma
C) Meghna
D) Karnaphuli
CORRECT ANSWER: A) Brahmaputra/Jamuna, Dharla, and Teesta

[MCQ] Kurigram shares a border with...
A) India (Assam and West Bengal)
B) Myanmar
C) Nepal
D) Bhutan
CORRECT ANSWER: A) India

[MCQ] "Chilmari" is famous for its historical...
A) River Port
B) Airport
C) Tea garden
D) Coal mine
CORRECT ANSWER: A) River Port

[TRUE/FALSE] The "Chilmari River Port" was a strategic point during the Liberation War and British era.
CORRECT ANSWER: True

[MCQ] "Bhurungamari" upazila is at the border.
A) True
B) False
CORRECT ANSWER: A) True

[MCQ] "Dharla Bridge" is a notable infrastructure in Kurigram.
A) True
B) False
CORRECT ANSWER: A) True

[TRUE/FALSE] Kurigram is prone to annual floods due to its many rivers.
CORRECT ANSWER: True

[MCQ] "Naodanga Zamindar Bari" is a historic site in Kurigram.
A) True
B) False
CORRECT ANSWER: A) True

A) Bhawaiya songs

A) True
B) False

[MCQ] "Monga" (seasonal famine) was historically associated with this region but has reduced.
A) True
B) False

[TRUE/FALSE] Handloom and cottage industries exist in rural areas.

A) 350 km
B) 450 km
