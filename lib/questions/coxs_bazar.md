=== COX'S BAZAR - RE-CATEGORIZED AND EXPANDED QUIZ ===

Category: Places & History (Minimum 15 Questions)
Historical sites, landmarks, and the rich heritage of Cox's Bazar.

[MCQ] Cox's Bazar is famous for having the world's...
A) Tallest building
B) Longest natural sea beach
C) Largest lake
D) Deepest cave
CORRECT ANSWER: B) Longest natural sea beach

[TRUE/FALSE] Cox's Bazar beach is approximately 120 kilometers (75 miles) long, making it the longest unbroken sea beach in the world.
CORRECT ANSWER: True

[MCQ] Cox's Bazar was named after...
A) A Mughal emperor
B) Captain Hiram Cox, a British officer
C) A local sultan
D) A Portuguese explorer
CORRECT ANSWER: B) Captain Hiram Cox, a British officer

[MCQ] The "Himchari National Park" is located near...
A) Teknaf
B) Cox's Bazar town
C) Ramu
D) Ukhiya
CORRECT ANSWER: B) Cox's Bazar town

[TRUE/FALSE] Himchari is famous for its waterfalls and hilly terrain.
CORRECT ANSWER: True

[MCQ] "Inani Beach" is known for its...
A) Black sand
B) Rocky coral stones and clear water
C) Mangrove forest
D) Mudflats
CORRECT ANSWER: B) Rocky coral stones and clear water

[MCQ] The "Maheshkhali Island" is famous for which temple?
A) Kantaji Temple
B) Adinath Temple
C) Dhakeshwari Temple
D) Jessoreswari Temple
CORRECT ANSWER: B) Adinath Temple

[TRUE/FALSE] Maheshkhali Island is the only hilly island in Bangladesh.
CORRECT ANSWER: True

[MCQ] "St. Martin's Island," the only coral island of Bangladesh, is located in which district?
A) Chittagong
B) Patuakhali
C) Cox's Bazar
D) Bhola
CORRECT ANSWER: C) Cox's Bazar

[MCQ] The local name for St. Martin's Island is...
A) Sonadia
B) Narikel Jinjira (Coconut Island)
C) Nijhum Dwip
D) Chera Dwip
CORRECT ANSWER: B) Narikel Jinjira (Coconut Island)

[TRUE/FALSE] "Teknaf" is the southernmost point of mainland Bangladesh.
CORRECT ANSWER: True

[MCQ] The "Ramu" upazila is famous for its...
A) Tea gardens
B) Buddhist temples and monasteries
C) Hills
D) Islamic heritage
CORRECT ANSWER: B) Buddhist temples and monasteries

[MCQ] The "Aggameda Khyang" is a famous...
A) Hindu temple
B) Buddhist monastery
C) Mosque
D) Church
CORRECT ANSWER: B) Buddhist monastery

[TRUE/FALSE] Cox's Bazar was previously known as "Panowa" or "Palongkee."
CORRECT ANSWER: True

[MCQ] "Sonadia Island" is known for its...
A) Coral reefs
B) Dried fish industry and migratory birds
C) Hills
D) Dense forests
CORRECT ANSWER: B) Dried fish industry and migratory birds

[MCQ] The "Dulahazra Safari Park" is located near...
A) Teknaf
B) Chakaria
C) Maheshkhali
D) Ramu
CORRECT ANSWER: B) Chakaria

[TRUE/FALSE] Dulahazra Safari Park features both local and African wildlife.
CORRECT ANSWER: True

[MCQ] The "Shah Omir Mosque" in Ramu is a historic...
A) Sultanate-era mosque
B) Mughal-era mosque
C) British-era building
D) Modern construction
CORRECT ANSWER: A) Sultanate-era mosque

Category: Culture & Traditions (Minimum 15 Questions)
Local customs, festivals, food, and the cultural identity of Cox's Bazar.

[MCQ] The "Rakhine" community in Cox's Bazar has a distinct cultural heritage originating from...
A) India
B) Myanmar (Burma)
C) Thailand
D) China
CORRECT ANSWER: B) Myanmar (Burma)

[TRUE/FALSE] The Rakhine people are predominantly Buddhist.
CORRECT ANSWER: True

[MCQ] Which dish is Cox's Bazar famous for?
A) Biryani
B) Mezban
C) Shutki (Dried Fish)
D) Pitha
CORRECT ANSWER: C) Shutki (Dried Fish)

[MCQ] The "Rakhine Rash Mela" is a festival celebrated by...
A) Hindus
B) Muslims
C) Buddhists
D) Christians
CORRECT ANSWER: C) Buddhists

[TRUE/FALSE] Dried fish (Shutki) from Cox's Bazar is exported to many countries.
CORRECT ANSWER: True

[MCQ] Which traditional handicraft is associated with the Rakhine community?
A) Jamdani weaving
B) Handloom weaving (Rakhine textiles)
C) Pottery
D) Blacksmithing
CORRECT ANSWER: B) Handloom weaving (Rakhine textiles)

[MCQ] "Lobster" and "Crab" are popular seafood in Cox's Bazar. What is the local term for crab?
A) Kakra
B) Chingri
C) Ilish
D) Koral
CORRECT ANSWER: A) Kakra

[TRUE/FALSE] The annual "Sea Beach Cultural Festival" is held in Cox's Bazar.
CORRECT ANSWER: True

[MCQ] The "Chakma" indigenous community also lives in parts of Cox's Bazar, particularly in...
A) Maheshkhali
B) Teknaf and border areas
C) St. Martin's
D) Sonadia
CORRECT ANSWER: B) Teknaf and border areas

[MCQ] Which fish is abundantly found in the Bay of Bengal near Cox's Bazar?
A) Salmon
B) Hilsa, Pomfret, and various marine fish
C) Trout
D) Catfish only
CORRECT ANSWER: B) Hilsa, Pomfret, and various marine fish

[TRUE/FALSE] "Ngappi" is a traditional fermented shrimp paste used by the Rakhine community.
CORRECT ANSWER: True

[MCQ] The "Toddy" or palm wine is traditionally made by which community in the region?
A) Bengalis
B) Rakhines and local toddy tappers
C) Marmas
D) Chakmas
CORRECT ANSWER: B) Rakhines and local toddy tappers

[MCQ] Which traditional boat is used by fishermen in Cox's Bazar?
A) Kosha Nauka
B) Sampan
C) Bhatiyali Nauka
D) Launch
CORRECT ANSWER: B) Sampan

[TRUE/FALSE] Cox's Bazar hosts one of the largest floating fish markets in the country.
CORRECT ANSWER: True (at Teknaf and other fishing villages)

[MCQ] Which Buddhist festival involves water throwing and is celebrated by the Rakhine community?
A) Wesak
B) Sangrai (similar to Thai Songkran)
C) Bodhi Day
D) Uposatha
CORRECT ANSWER: B) Sangrai (similar to Thai Songkran)

Category: Everyday Bangladesh (Minimum 15 Questions)
Modern life, infrastructure, and current significance of Cox's Bazar.

[MCQ] Cox's Bazar Airport is located in...
A) Teknaf
B) Cox's Bazar Sadar
C) Ramu
D) Chakaria
CORRECT ANSWER: B) Cox's Bazar Sadar

[TRUE/FALSE] Cox's Bazar is one of the most popular tourist destinations in Bangladesh.
CORRECT ANSWER: True

[MCQ] The "Marine Drive Road" connects Cox's Bazar to...
A) Chittagong
B) Teknaf (along the coast)
C) Dhaka
D) Sylhet
CORRECT ANSWER: B) Teknaf (along the coast)

[MCQ] Which major development project aims to improve infrastructure in Cox's Bazar?
A) Metro Rail
B) Cox's Bazar Development Authority projects
C) Space Research Center
D) Mining project
CORRECT ANSWER: B) Cox's Bazar Development Authority projects

[TRUE/FALSE] The Rohingya refugee camps are located in Cox's Bazar district.
CORRECT ANSWER: True (primarily in Ukhiya and Teknaf)

[MCQ] The "Radiant Fish World" is a...
A) Fish market
B) Aquarium and marine theme park
C) Fishing company
D) Research institute
CORRECT ANSWER: B) Aquarium and marine theme park

[MCQ] Which hotel chain has luxury resorts in Cox's Bazar?
A) Multiple local and international chains
B) Only government hotels
C) No hotels
D) Only budget hotels
CORRECT ANSWER: A) Multiple local and international chains

[TRUE/FALSE] Cox's Bazar has a railway connection.
CORRECT ANSWER: False (Railway is under construction)

[MCQ] The distance from Dhaka to Cox's Bazar by road is approximately...
A) 150 km
B) 250 km
C) 400 km
D) 600 km
CORRECT ANSWER: C) 400 km (approx. 390-400 km)

[MCQ] "Kolatoli" is a famous area in Cox's Bazar known for...
A) Industrial zone
B) Hotels, restaurants, and tourist activities
C) Agricultural land
D) Fishing port
CORRECT ANSWER: B) Hotels, restaurants, and tourist activities

[TRUE/FALSE] Paragliding and horse riding are popular activities on Cox's Bazar beach.
CORRECT ANSWER: True

[MCQ] The "Burmese Market" in Cox's Bazar sells...
A) Only food
B) Imported goods from Myanmar, textiles, and souvenirs
C) Only electronics
D) Only medicines
CORRECT ANSWER: B) Imported goods from Myanmar, textiles, and souvenirs

[MCQ] Which organization manages the Rohingya refugee response in Cox's Bazar?
A) Only Bangladesh government
B) UNHCR and other international agencies with Bangladesh government
C) Only NGOs
D) No organization
CORRECT ANSWER: B) UNHCR and other international agencies with Bangladesh government

[TRUE/FALSE] Cox's Bazar has a dedicated "Tourism Police" unit.
CORRECT ANSWER: True

[MCQ] "Laboni Point" is a famous...
A) Mountain peak
B) Beach point/viewpoint in Cox's Bazar
C) Island
D) Cave
CORRECT ANSWER: B) Beach point/viewpoint in Cox's Bazar

[MCQ] The "Cox's Bazar Government College" is a major educational institution in the district.
A) True
B) False
CORRECT ANSWER: A) True
